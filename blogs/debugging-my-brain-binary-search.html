Debugging My Brain: How Binary Search Changed How I Think

October 14, 2025

I used to brute-force everything—both in code and in life. Learning binary search taught me to eliminate half of the noise every time. Now, when I study or make business decisions, I think in O(log n).

When I'm debugging code, I don't check every line anymore. I start in the middle, check if the bug occurs before or after that point, and eliminate half the codebase. What used to take an hour now takes 10 minutes.

The same principle applies to my Amazon FBA business. Instead of analyzing products one by one, I set threshold criteria and cut the list in half immediately. Binary search isn't just an algorithm—it's a way of thinking that makes everything more efficient.
